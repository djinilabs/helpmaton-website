We’ve all been there: Monday morning arrives, and before you can even touch your "real" work, you’re stuck in a loop of tab-switching. You’re checking Google News for industry updates, pulling metrics from HubSpot, scanning Notion for project statuses, and scrolling through Slack to see what you missed over the weekend.

By the time you’ve "caught up," you’ve burned your best hours of deep work on manual data retrieval.

At **Helpmaton**, we believe AI shouldn’t just be a chatbot you talk to—it should be a teammate that works for you even when you aren't around. Using **Agent Schedules**, you can move from manual "pulling" of information to automated "pushing" of insights.

Here are five recurring reports you can set up in minutes to reclaim your time.

---

### 1. The Friday Afternoon "Market Pulse"

**The Goal:** Stay ahead of industry shifts without the "doomscrolling."

Keeping up with industry trends is vital, but it’s often the first thing to drop when things get busy. With Helpmaton’s **Web Search & Content Extraction** tool, you can build an agent that acts as your dedicated researcher.

* **The Setup:** Create an agent in your "Marketing Intelligence" workspace. Schedule it to run every Friday at 3:00 PM.
* **The Workflow:** The agent performs a targeted web search for keywords like "Generative AI updates" or "SaaS pricing trends," extracts the text from relevant articles, and filters out the noise.
* **The Delivery:** It delivers a bulleted summary to your team’s Slack channel, categorizing news into "Must Reads" and "General Updates."

### 2. Daily Executive KPI Summaries

**The Goal:** Know your numbers before your first meeting starts.

Opening a heavy BI dashboard or a complex CRM every morning is a friction point. Using Helpmaton’s **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** integrations, your agents can talk directly to tools like **HubSpot, Salesforce, or PostHog**.

* **The Setup:** Set an "Analyst Agent" to trigger every morning at 8:00 AM.
* **The Workflow:** The agent pulls data on yesterday’s new leads, closed deals, or website conversion rates.
* **The Delivery:** A concise summary is sent to your email or a private Discord DM. If a metric is significantly higher or lower than the 7-day average, the agent flags it as an "Anomaly Alert."

### 3. Bi-Weekly Competitor "Shadow" Reports

**The Goal:** Track what the competition is doing—not just what they’re saying.

The most powerful feature of Helpmaton is **Agent Memory**. Unlike standard AI bots that forget everything once a chat ends, Helpmaton agents remember previous interactions.

* **The Setup:** Schedule a "Competitor Tracker" agent to visit your top three competitors' pricing and "What’s New" pages every twond and fourth Tuesday.
* **The Workflow:** The agent compares the current page content with the version it saved in its memory from two weeks ago.
* **The Delivery:** Instead of a full page dump, the agent only reports the *differences*—like a $10 price hike or a new feature announcement—and saves the update to a shared **Notion** database.

### 4. Monday Morning Project Rollups

**The Goal:** Eliminate the "What are we doing today?" confusion.

Status meetings are often just people reading things that are already documented. You can automate this by connecting Helpmaton to **Linear, GitHub, or Notion**.

* **The Setup:** Schedule a "Project Manager Agent" for Monday at 7:30 AM.
* **The Workflow:** The agent scans your project boards to see which tasks were "Completed" last week and which are currently "In Progress" or "Blocked."
* **The Delivery:** A structured report is posted to the team’s #project-updates channel, highlighting blocked tasks that need immediate attention.

### 5. The 24-Hour Community Sentiment Digest

**The Goal:** Understand the "vibe" of your community without reading 500 messages.

If you manage a busy Slack or Discord community, it’s easy to miss the forest for the trees. Helpmaton’s native **Chat Platform Bots** can monitor specific channels 24/7.

* **The Setup:** Create a "Sentiment Agent" that triggers every evening at 6:00 PM.
* **The Workflow:** The agent reviews the day’s conversations, using **Custom Summaries** to distill hours of chat into a few key takeaways.
* **The Delivery:** A digest highlighting the top 3 most-discussed topics, any recurring complaints, and a "Sentiment Score" (e.g., "The community is feeling frustrated with the recent login bug").
